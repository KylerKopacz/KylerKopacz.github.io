Career Goals

Right now, I feel like there are three paths that I could take in the computer science world:

Systems/Low-Level

One thing that I have really come to appreciate is low-level systems programming. After taking a systems course at Amherst College, I have seen low-level programming as a very precise and efficient way of programming that takes requires a great attention to detail. Something about being asked to writeefficient code that runs under a layer of abstraction that most consumers won't see calls to me. I think it would be incredbile to write the fast and error-free code that powers autonomous vehicles, self-landing rockets, the latest smartphones, or a new and powerful computer architecture. To prepare myself for this potential career, I have worked with C/C++ to write emulators and other projects, but there is still a long way to go.

Web Development

Web Development is everywhere in today's world. Every company has a presence online, and for a company to really stand out from the competition, it needs a slick, fast, and clean website. There are so many different purposes for websites as well. There are websites for commerce, portfolios, marketing, crowd-funding, social media, and many more. I think that it would be very cool to learn all there is to know about web design and work to build sleek and powerful web applications that allow companies to get ahead. Web Dev also allows my work to be showcased to the world. It would be amazing to impact people around the world with software that I wrote and put on the web.

Machine Learning

AI is booming. Everywhere you look, you have companies pouring billions of dollars into AI research and applications. Over the past few years we have seen AI do things that were impossible only 10 years ago, and the field is only getting started. I have seen what AI can do and how it can make a huge impact on people's lives, and I think it would be amazing to be a trailblazer in the field of AI. Being able to solve complex problems with AI will push our civilization to a new level. AI can help us analyze and solve problems that humans alone could not. The autonomous world would allow humans to focus on other things and allow even more growth for society. My hope is to one day work with AI and implement intelligent systems that can learn to solve problems. To get there, I will be double majoring in Math at Amherst College to build the mathematical foundations necessary to succeed in the AI world.
